Professional Lawn Care & Mowing Services

We offer weekly lawn mowing as an exclusive seasonal contract, available only to our current customers when combined with any of our other maintenance services. We no longer offer bi-weekly or one-time cuts, as we believe that consistent weekly mowing is essential for optimal lawn health. Mowing begins in early May and continues through early November, coinciding with the growth cycle before lawns enter dormancy.

What our lawn mowing service includes:

  • Mow all lawn areas using commercial-grade mowers
  • Trim lawn edges
  • Blow off excess grass and clippings from walkways, patios, and driveways


At Meyer Landscapes, we take great pride in delivering exceptional lawn care that fosters healthy, vibrant grass. To achieve the best possible results for both your lawn and our services, we work closely with customers who share our dedication to quality and care.

Here are some factors that we consider before entering into a seasonal agreement with any of our lawn mowing customers:

  • Proper Hydration:
    An irrigation system is in place (or one is planned for installation this season) to ensure your lawn receives the necessary moisture to thrive all season long.

  • Targeted Treatments:
    A seasonal treatment plan, either self-managed or provided by one of the Cape's local fertilization specialists, is in place for overall lawn health and appearance.

  • Optimal Growth:
    Your lawn growth is consistent enough to warrant a weekly mow

  • Comprehensive Care:
    You utilize (or plan to utilize) Meyer Landscapes for at least one of our other landscape services.

Professional Landscape Maintenance

Seasonal Cleanups • Mulching • Pruning & Trimming • Weeding & Edging

When it comes to your yard, we take its health and appearance very seriously. Yard maintenance requires attention throughout each season, and it is not something that should just be done once a year. Check out our popular maintenance package options, catered to work with any budget.

What does a typical landscape season look like on Cape Cod?
We start the season with Spring cleanups, which focus on cleaning and weeding your planting beds as needed, and then re-edging the beds to prepare for mulch (by request). It is also important that pruning or thinning of herbaceous plantings, as well as deadwood removal be taken care of at this time. In the Spring, plants are coming out of dormancy and will respond very well to this treatment. We finish your cleanup by dethatching all the lawn areas and blowing off any debris.

Mulching follows shortly after the cleanup and is applied to all planting beds at a depth of no more than 2”, with extra care taken to avoid “mulch mounding” around the base of plantings; this can cause rotting to occur. Regular mulching of planting beds aids in moisture retention and keeps root systems cool and insulated during hot summer months.
Throughout the Summer season (a.k.a. the “growing” season), a couple of visits are usually recommended to perform regular pruning and trimming of plants, weeding, cleaning of beds, and checking on overall landscape health.

Fall cleanups signify the season's end with removal of leaves and debris from all lawn and planting bed areas, cleaning of first floor gutters as needed, and removal of deadwood from perennials. The start of this service varies year-to-year depending on temperature and when the leaves begin to drop. On average, we begin at the end of November. A good fall cleanup not only gets your property in shape and ready for winter, but also eliminates damage caused by leaves left decaying upon lawn areas.

How does a maintenance package work?

You let us know which maintenance package you are interested in, and we will prepare a contract catered to your property that covers all those services. The total cost will then be divided into nine (9) equal monthly payments invoiced on the 1st of each month, beginning April 1st and ending on December 31st.
